The AI-Powered Automated Sentiment Analysis Tool Project

This project is an ongoing research (Senior Thesis) by Ian Raymund Manaog, Kevin Kyle Hendrick Baldo and Levi John Sta. Ana. It aims to build an automated tool for detecting sentiment in Filipino-English Tweets using machine learning algorithms such as Artificial Neural Network, Support Vector Machines and Naive Bayes Classifiers.

Official Labeling Protocol & Guidelines for Volunteers

The 10 Commandments for Labelling

For the consistency and uniformity of the procedures used in labelling the dataset, the following rules must be applied when labelling a tweet:

  1. Rule 1 (Perspectivity): Tweets must be labelled based on the sentiment expressed by the user and not of the labeller. The question that is asked is, "How does the author of the tweet seem to feel based on the content of his post?"
  2. Rule 2 (Neutrality on Unclarity): Unclear posts such as ones that express incomplete ideas or fragmented thoughts must be labelled neutral.
  3. Rule 3 (Subject Dependency): Without the involvement of a third party subject or object such as a idea, ideology, company, product or service, the subject of the post is primarily the author and his expressions. However, if the author is creating content in association to an idea, ideology, company, product or service that does not necessarily involve his direct person or emotion, the subject becomes the user's perception of the service and not necessarily his emotions.
  4. Rule 4 (Negativity on Humorous Dissatisfaction with Service): If a post contains a review of a product or service but with a remark of humor or satire that is against the targeted product, the label must still be negative.
  5. Rule 5 (Negativity on Sarcasm): If a post contains a sarcastic remark even with a positive undertone, it is deemed negative as per Rule #3 wherein the user associates the content to another subject through his perspective.
  6. Rule 6 (Neutrality on Fragmented Post Due to Links, Images, Videos, etc.): If a post contains content beyond text that are not the scope of this research, it must be labelled neutral UNLESS it is otherwise obvious in the caption that the user is happy (positive remarks). However, if it is not obviously stated by the user, the tweet is labelled neutral.
  7. Rule 7 (Neutrality on Promotional Material and Posts): If a post contains content such advertisements, news, announcements and responses from service providers or sellers, the post must be labelled neutral if it is under a professional tone that imitates the form of script-like responses from company. If it is however on a casual tone, it can be labelled positive or negative accordingly.
  8. Rule 8 (Discarding of Non-Filipino English Tweets): Any tweet written partially or entirely in foreign language or a specific Filipino dialect must be discarded to reduce the noise in the data and to limit the scope of the dataset.
  9. Rule 9 (Discarding Tweets without Substantial Content): Any tweet that has only link, usernames, hashtags without any clear expression of reaction from the author should be marked for discarding.
  10. FINAL RULE . Rule 10 (The Use of Emojis to Identify Emotion): Emojis can be used to identify the emotions presented in the tweet but they must be regarded carefully, some tweets have a lot of positive emojis but with a negative undertone. In this case emojis are just a secondary source to the actual meaning of the text and must be looked upon together with the whole tweet and not just as a part.
